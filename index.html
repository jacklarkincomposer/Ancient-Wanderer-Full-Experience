Sparse · Searching

The Rune in His Palm

"It always looks ordinary, at first."

The stone warms in his hand before he even opens his fist to look at it. Something in it is pulling — not urgently, not yet, but with the quiet insistence of a compass finding north. He stands on the ridge and looks down into the valley below, at the village half-hidden in morning mist, at the smoke rising from chimneys and the distant movement of people going about their morning.

He closes his fingers around the stone and starts walking down.

Ancient · Steady

A Curse Doesn't Announce Itself

"It settles. It makes itself at home slowly, quietly."

He has been doing this long enough to know that a curse doesn't announce itself. It doesn't hang in the air like smoke or drip from the walls like rot. It settles — until the people inside it can't remember what life felt like before.

He goes south along the ridge path, then down through pine forest, then onto the old stone road that leads into the valley. The road is cracked but maintained. Someone still cares about this place. Or something does.

Warmth · Recognition

From the Ridgeline

"From up here the village looks peaceful. He has learned not to trust that."

The full folk world arrives — dulcimer, lutes, harp layering over the strings, a solo voice rising through it. The music carries the texture of a place that remembers what it was before whatever settled here made it forget.

Threshold · Stillness

The First Sign Is Always the Silence

"Not an absence of sound. But where the hum of human life should be — nothing."

Dogs barking somewhere. A cart wheel turning. The distant ring of something metal. But underneath all of it, where the hum of human life should be, there is nothing. No laughter. No argument. No children shouting at each other across a street.

He adjusts the satchel on his hip and walks through the gate.

Wonder · Unsettling

The Hollow Market

"The stalls are set up. The fires lit. Nobody is buying or selling."

The market is open. That is the strangest part. A woman stands behind a vegetable stall looking at nothing. A man carries a bundle of wood, sets it down, stands still. A child sits on a doorstep staring at the ground between her feet.

They are all simply present — occupying their usual positions in the usual routines of the day, with nothing behind their eyes. He has seen this before. He knows what it is.

Belonging · Journey

The Pull at the Centre

"He finds himself wondering how long they have been like this."

He walks through the square slowly. A few of the hollow faces turn to follow him — not with recognition, not with suspicion, just with the dull mechanical tracking of eyes that still function even when the person behind them has gone somewhere else.

Long enough that this feels like normal. He checks the stone. Still warm. Still pulling him toward the centre of the village.

Ominous · Ancient

A Theory About Curses

"Something in a place opens a door. And the things that live in the dark spaces come through it."

They are not random. They do not fall on places by accident. Something calls them. Something in a place — a wrong done and never righted, a grief that curdled into something else, an old compact broken and forgotten — opens a door.

He doesn't know who opened the door here. It doesn't matter. His job is not to understand. His job is to close it.

Dread · Emergence

The Thing at the Centre

"It looks at him the way old things look at things that dare to be in their way."

The ground cracks before he hears anything. A split running through the old cobblestones from somewhere beneath, spreading outward like a fracture in ice. And then the thing comes up through the crack.

It is old. Older than the village, older than the road he walked in on. Dark roots and shadow and the slow patient malice of something that has been waiting underground for a very long time. Two red eyes open in the mass of it. He draws his sword.

Chaos · Full Force

The Fight

"He has fought things like this before and it is never clean."

The roots move faster than they look like they should. The darkness absorbs the light around it, making the square feel smaller and stranger than it is. It catches him twice — once across the ribs, once across the shoulder — and both times he keeps moving because stopping is how you lose.

The villagers stand and watch with their empty eyes. Some part of him wonders if they can see it at all.

Gravity · Spent

The Fall

"He presses the advantage until there isn't anything left to press against."

He gets beneath it eventually. Low and fast, inside the reach of the roots, where the core of it lives. The sword connects with something that resists and then gives. The red eyes flicker. The roots slow.

Then he falls to his knees.

Stillness · Grief

The Quiet After

"He has always wondered whether anyone who came after him would ever know what happened here."

He is on the ground. The square is quiet again, quieter than before, and the villagers are starting to stir — not waking exactly, not yet, but something in them is shifting. The absence that has been sitting on this place is beginning to lift.

The stone in his hand is burning hot now, almost too hot to hold. He holds it anyway. Probably the latter. It usually is.

Resolution · Gold

The Sun Returns

"The rune dissolves in his hand. Gold light drifting upward like embers."

He raises the stone above his head and holds it there. The gold comes slowly at first — a warmth spreading from the inside, the red bleeding outward as the gold takes its place. Then the beam, straight up through the overcast sky.

The roots wither. The shadow retreats. Around him, one by one, the villagers blink. Really blink. A woman makes a small confused sound. A child starts crying without knowing why. Task complete.
