Sparse · Departure

He Doesn't Look Back

"The aftermath is not for him."

He doesn't stay for what comes next. He has learned, over time, that the aftermath is not for him — the confusion and the grief and the slow piecing together of what happened belongs to the people it happened to. His presence in it would only complicate things.

Behind him he can hear the village waking up. Voices — real ones this time, with weight and confusion and feeling behind them. Someone calling a name. Someone else answering. He doesn't look back.

Walking · Northward

Forge Smoke on the Ridge

"A settlement. That will do."

He thinks he saw forge smoke from the ridge, to the north. A settlement. That will do.

He picks up his broken sword, sheathes it, and walks toward the gate. The Birbyne and the Shepard flute step in over the harp; a quiet choir gathers behind the strings as the road begins.

Heat · Arrival

First Sight of the Forge Village

"After the silence of the cursed village this feels like breathing again."

He smells the settlement before he sees it — coal smoke and hot metal and the particular sharpness of quenched steel. Then he crests the hill and there it is below him, built into the hillside like it grew there, heavy Norse stone and iron and the glow of a dozen forges burning orange in the grey afternoon.

He can hear it from up here. Hammers and voices and the hiss of steam. Life, actual life, going about its business without anything wrong underneath it. He starts down the hill.

Bustle · Full Forge

A Place Too Busy to Worry

"A cloaked traveller with a blade to repair is not an unusual sight in a place like this."

The forge settlement has the particular energy of a place that has always been too busy to worry about anything beyond the next order. People move with purpose here. They argue over prices and shout across the street at each other and laugh at jokes he doesn't catch. Nobody looks at him twice.

He finds the smith by reputation, asking twice and following pointed directions through two narrow streets and down a set of stone steps into a workshop built low into the hill, warm and loud and smelling of iron.

Stripped · Waiting

An Hour

"He pays without negotiating. The smith seems almost disappointed by this."

He lays the blade on the workbench without ceremony. The smith picks it up, turns it over, runs a thumb along the damaged edge with the practiced indifference of someone who has seen a thousand damaged blades and found most of them less interesting than their owners believed.

The smith names a price. The Wanderer pays it without negotiating. The smith says it will be an hour. The Wanderer nods and sits down on a low bench by the wall to wait.

Recognition · Quiet

The Rune on the Shelf

"He recognises it immediately. He has no word for what it is. He has never needed one."

That is when he sees it. On the shelf behind the smith, between a pair of iron tongs and a collection of small decorative metalwork pieces, sits a rune. The carved stone, the central symbol, the circle of pictograms around the edge — glowing faintly red in the firelight.

Beside it, a hand-lettered price sign. The number written on it is large enough that he reads it twice. Someone found this. Didn't know what it was. Sold it to a curiosity collector. And it has been sitting on this shelf ever since, glowing quietly, pointing toward someone who needs help and going nowhere.

Decision · Stillness

He Is Not a Man Who Struggles With Decisions

"The smith values it as an object. He needs it as a key."

He waits. The smith works the blade at the far end of the bench, back turned, focused entirely on the steel. The rune sits on the shelf. The Wanderer sits on the bench. The fire pops and hisses.

Someone is waiting for that rune. Possibly has been waiting for it for however long it has been sitting on this shelf. The smith values it as an object. He needs it as a key. The smith turns back to the grindstone.

Pulse · Theft

Two Seconds

"They simply look at each other. Then he is already moving."

He moves quickly. Hand across the bench, fingers around the stone, into the cloak. Done. The smith turns around.

There is a moment — maybe two seconds — where they simply look at each other. Then the smith looks at the shelf. Then back at him. Then opens his mouth. He is already moving.

Chase · Full Pursuit

Not Dignified

"Extremely good at leaving places in a hurry."

The chase through the forge settlement is not dignified. He runs through the narrow stone streets with the smith's voice behind him raising the alarm, past workers who turn to look and then dive aside, past a cart that tips as he rounds a corner too fast, past two guards who are too slow off the mark.

He takes a set of steps three at a time, ducks through an arch, breaks left onto a wider street and then immediately right into an alley, through a gap between two buildings that a broader man couldn't manage, and suddenly he is outside the settlement entirely, on open ground, the sounds of pursuit already fading behind him.

Forest · The Rune

He Opens His Hand

"He has left things in worse places."

He walks into the trees and keeps walking until the forge settlement is gone entirely and there is only forest around him, old growth, the kind that has been here long enough to stop caring about the noise of men.

He stops. Catches his breath — more ragged than he would like, a reminder that the fight in the cursed village cost him more than he has been willing to admit. The sword is still on the smith's workbench. He won't be going back for it. He has left things in worse places. He opens his hand and looks at the rune.
