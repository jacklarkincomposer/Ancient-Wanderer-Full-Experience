The Ancient Wanderer

A Final Year Project — Jack Larkin, 2026

The Ancient Wanderer is an audiovisual experience built around the idea that music should be moved through, not just heard. The score is written in three chapters — three distinct sound worlds — each one unfolding as you scroll, layering stem by stem from near silence into something full.

The project grew out of a frustration with how composed music is usually presented: static, front-loaded, asking the listener to sit still and receive. I wanted to write something that rewarded pace and attention, where the listener's movement through the page was itself a kind of performance.

Each chapter is built around a place: a cursed village, a forge, a fishing harbour. The music for each was composed to have a spatial quality — sounds that feel like they come from a world rather than a speaker. Stems are introduced progressively as the listener moves deeper into the scene, with ambience, melody, and rhythm arriving in sequence rather than all at once.

Technically, the experience is built in vanilla JavaScript and Web Audio API, with a custom engine handling stem crossfades, scroll-triggered playback, and per-section gain curves. Everything runs in the browser with no frameworks.
